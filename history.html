Historical Sites

Discover Argao's rich history through its well-preserved Spanish-era churches, ancestral houses, and centuries-old watchtowers. Stroll these streets to experience local traditions, architectural heritage, and stories that reveal the town's unique cultural identity.

St. Michael the Archangel Church

Built between 1734 and 1788, this Baroque-Rococo church is one of Cebu’s finest Spanish-era structures. It features coral-stone walls, intricate carvings, and beautiful ceiling murals. It is declared a National Historical Landmark.

Puerta Marina

Puerta Marina is an 18th-century stone gate built during the Spanish era to protect Argao from Moro raids. Made of coral stone and lime, it served as a defensive entrance facing the sea and remains a well-preserved symbol of the town’s colonial past.

Mount Lantoy

Mount Lantoy, rising about 593 meters above sea level, is a natural landmark and part of Argao’s historical and cultural identity. It is linked to local folklore, particularly the legend of Maria Cacao, a diwata said to reside in a cave within the mountain. She is believed to be the guardian of cacao plantations and a symbol of prosperity and nature’s generosity. The mountain remains both a historical and mythological treasure of the town.
