Events

Experience Argao’s vibrant events, from colorful religious festivals and lively beach gatherings to traditional music, food fairs, and cultural performances that celebrate the town’s heritage and community spirit.

Bangkarera

Bangkarera in Argao is usually held every September as part of the La Torta Festival celebration. The event takes place along Argao’s coastal areas, bringing together local fishermen and spectators to enjoy the exciting boat races. It adds a lively maritime highlight to the town’s festivities, which honor both Argao’s seafaring heritage and its famous torta delicacy.

Miss Argao Competition

Miss Argao is a prestigious beauty pageant held during the La Torta Festival in September. It showcases the beauty, intelligence, and talent of young Argawanon women who serve as ambassadors of the town’s culture and values. The event highlights not only physical grace but also the contestants’ knowledge of Argao’s heritage, traditions, and community pride. It is one of the most anticipated parts of the festival, drawing large crowds and celebrating the elegance and empowerment of Argao’s women.

Torta Eating Contest

Torta Eating Contest in Argao, Cebu is a fun and lively event held during the La Torta Festival. Participants compete to eat as many of Argao’s famous torta cakes as they can within a set time. The contest highlights the town’s pride in its beloved delicacy while providing entertainment for both locals and visitors. It’s a joyful celebration of Argao’s culinary tradition and community spirit.

Pitgalong Festival

The Pitlagong Festival honors Argao’s patron saint, Michael the Archangel. The festival is celebrated through a parade, a street dancing competition, and a ritual showdown. The term pitlagong is a bamboo instrument used to clean the sugong. The sugong is a bamboo container used in collecting tuba, or coconut wine. Tuba is a traditional Filipino drink and it is believed that the tuba will not taste good without the pitlagong.

La Torta Festival

La Torta Festival is a yearly celebration in Argao, Cebu, held every September to honor the town’s patron saint, St. Michael the Archangel. The festival highlights Argao’s famous local delicacy, the torta, a sweet and fluffy sponge cake made with tuba (coconut wine). The event features colorful parades, cultural dances, a torta-making and eating contest, and various fun competitions that showcase Argao’s rich heritage, craftsmanship, and joyful community spirit.

Argao Skimboarding Competition

Skimboarding Event in Argao, Cebu is a thrilling competition that draws local surfers and beach enthusiasts to the town’s scenic shoreline. Participants showcase their agility and balance as they glide across the shallow waves on their skimboards, performing tricks and smooth maneuvers. The event celebrates Argao’s coastal lifestyle and youthful energy, promoting sportsmanship and tourism.
